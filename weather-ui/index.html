Time outdoor activities wisely

Avoid going out during the hottest parts of the day (between 11 a.m. and 4 p.m.). If you do go out, protect yourself from direct sun heat by wearing a hat or an umbrella.

Stay Hydrated

Drink plenty of water and other hydrating fluids, such as coconut water, lemon water, and fresh fruit juices, to avoid dehydration. Caffeinated and alcoholic beverages should be avoided because they dehydrate the body.

Comfortable Clothing

Wear light-coloured, loose-fitting, and breathable clothing to stay cool and comfortable during the summer season. Avoid dark-coloured clothes, as they absorb heat and can make you feel uncomfortable. Silk, satin, leather, or clothes with brocade, lace, or net can cause skin irritation and rashes.

Apply Sunscreen

Before exposing your skin to the sun, it is important to apply sunscreen with a minimum SPF of 30 to shield yourself from harmful UV rays. ReapplyIf you are going to be outside for an extended period, reapply every two hours.
